Branches of Government

Executive Branch

Powers

Has the power to...

  • Command the Military
  • Veto a bill
  • Appoint Justices and department heads
  • Grant pardons
  • Make decrees and declarations
  • Wage war with the support of Congress

Checks

Legistlative Checks...

  • Impeachment by the House
  • Selection of the president and vice president in the event of no majority electoral votes
  • Senate approves some appointments
  • Treaties and ambassadors are approved by the senate

Judicial Checks...

  • Judicial review
  • Cheif Justice is the President of the Senate during impeachment

Legistlative Branch

Powers

Has the power to...

  • Wite and enact laws
  • Declare war
  • Deny presidental appointments
  • The Senate ratifies treaties
  • Override vetoes by the President
  • Start investigations against any branch, especially the executive
  • Sets budget (although the president is responsible for it) and taxes

Checks

Executive Checks...

  • The power to veto laws
  • Vice President is President of the Senate
  • Commands the military, dispite the power of war being in Congress
  • Call the congress to session in an emergency

Judicial Checks...

  • Judicial review
  • Seats are held only with good behavior

Judicial Branch

Powers

Has the power to...

  • Determine which laws are unconsitutional
  • Determine how laws apply to disputes
  • Determine how laws should be interpreted
  • Police its own members

Checks

Executive Checks...

  • Power to appoint judges
  • Pardon power

Legislative Checks...

  • Senate approves federal judges
  • Impeachment power
  • Power to initate consitutional ammendments
  • Power to set jurisdiction of courts
  • Power to change the size of courts

From the Constitution

Quoted from Article I of the Constitution

The judicial Power of the United States, shall be vested in one supreme Court, and in such inferior Courts as the Congress may from time to time ordain and establish. The Judges, both of the supreme and inferior Courts, shall hold their Offices during good Behaviour, and shall, at stated Times, receive for their Services, a Compensation, which shall not be diminished during their Continuance in Office...
